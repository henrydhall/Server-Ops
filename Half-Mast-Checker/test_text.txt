<p style="text-align: center;"><em><strong>POW/MIA flag to fly at federal sites year round</strong></em> &#8211; November 8, 2019</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><a href="https://www.congress.gov/bill/116th-congress/senate-bill/693">S.693 — 116th Congress (2019-2020) / Public Law No: 116-67 </a></p>
<p style="text-align: left;">Prominent federal buildings and national war memorials will now fly the iconic POW/MIA flag alongside the American flag throughout the year thanks to legislation signed into law.<br />
The proposal, passed without objection in the House and the Senate, it is designed to help highlight the continued sacrifice of military families whose loved ones are still unaccounted for overseas, estimated at about 82,000 individuals.</p>
<p style="text-align: left;">Advocates began pushing for the year-long display of the flag earlier this year after some lawmakers in Washington, D.C. stopped displaying the black and white “you are not forgotten” banner outside their congressional offices.<br />
<em><strong>The law applies only to a specific set of federal buildings, including the White House, U.S. Capitol, the Pentagon and Department of Veterans Affairs headquarters. Every post office throughout the country will also be required to fly the POW/MIA flag.</strong></em><br />
<em><strong>In addition, display of the flag will be required at every major U.S. military installation, every national cemetery, and numerous high-profile war-related sites like the World War II Memorial, and the Vietnam Veterans Memorial.</strong></em></p>
<p style="text-align: left;"><a href="https://www.warren.senate.gov/newsroom/press-releases/national-pow/mia-flag-act-signed-into-law">The proposal was sponsored by Sen. Elizabeth Warren, D-Mass., and Rep. Chris Pappas, D-N.H.</a></p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><em><strong>The daily display of the POW/MIA flag at all prominent federal properties serves as a daily reminder that these heroes, and their families are forever honored and never forgotten.</strong></em></p>
